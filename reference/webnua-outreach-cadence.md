# Webnua Perth — Outreach Cadence

**The engine that turns a researched prospect into a booked call.**

Built around the Proof Page. Designed to produce 1–2 booked calls a day at starting volume, and to scale to 3–5+ by turning one dial — volume — with no change to the cadence itself.

---

## The core principle

**The cadence is the engine. Volume is the throttle.**

You build the engine once, correctly. It converts a fixed percentage of whoever enters it. Want more booked calls? You don't redesign anything — you pour more researched prospects in the top. This document is the engine plus the throttle settings.

Two numbers to keep in your head:

- **Conversion target:** ~2% of prospects entered → booked call. (Cold outreach with a strong personalised artefact realistically lands 1–3%. We plan at 2% and treat 3% as upside.)
- **The throttle:** at 2%, every 50 researched prospects entered ≈ 1 booked call.

So the volume ladder is simply:

| Booked calls/day target | Prospects entered/day | Prospects entered/week |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | ~50 | ~250 |
| 2 | ~100 | ~500 |
| 3–5 | ~150–250 | ~750–1,250 |

Everything else in this document is fixed. Only that table moves.

---

## What has to be true before a prospect enters the cadence

A prospect is **not** ready for the cadence until all three exist:

1. **A completed audit** — run through the pipeline tool, Step 1. Real PageSpeed / GBP / Ad Library / local-search pull.
2. **A generated Proof Page** — Step 2 and 3 of the tool. Their branded version, the audit findings in Act 1, a real shareable link.
3. **Verified contact details** — at minimum a working email and mobile number. Owner first name confirmed, not guessed. A wrong name kills the whole thing.

If any of the three is missing, the prospect goes back to the prep queue, not into the cadence. The cadence assumes a real Proof Page link exists for every person in it.

## The shape of the cadence

**7 touches over 16 days. Three channels: email, SMS, engagement-triggered phone.**

The Proof Page link goes out in **touch 1** — the artefact is strong enough to open cold, so there's no "earn the open first" warm-up phase. Touch 1 *is* the opener. Everything after it is follow-through on what's already inside the page.

The phone call is **not** scheduled — it's **triggered**. It fires only when a prospect shows an engagement signal (opened the Proof Page, scrolled it, clicked the call link but didn't book). A call to someone who's already seen the page is a warm call. A cold dial to everyone is not the model and does not scale.

### The sequence at a glance

| # | Day | Channel | Purpose | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Deliver the Proof Page. The whole offer in one link. | All prospects |
| 2 | Day 1 | SMS | Short nudge — "sent you something, did it land?" | All prospects |
| 3 | Day 3 | Email | Surface ONE element from inside the page (the leak / the proof). | All who haven't booked |
| 4 | Day 5 | **Phone** | The warm call. | **Engagement signal only** |
| 5 | Day 8 | SMS | The guarantee angle — risk reversal in one line. | All who haven't booked |
| 6 | Day 12 | Email | The "before I close your file" pattern interrupt. | All who haven't booked |
| 7 | Day 16 | SMS | Final, genuinely-final one-liner. Then exit. | All who haven't booked |

**Non-engagers who reach the end of touch 7 are retired from the cadence.** They are not chased further. At the volume you need, time spent on a 16-day non-responder is time stolen from fresh prospects. Retired prospects can re-enter once, 90+ days later, with a fresh audit — never recycled with stale findings.

## The touches — every message templated

Fill-ins are marked `{like_this}`. They map to the audit + Proof Page fields:

### TOUCH 1 — Day 0 — Email — Deliver the Proof Page

The artefact carries the open. The email's only job is to get the click. Short. Specific. No pitch in the email body — the pitch *is* the page.

**Subject line — use the finding, not a pitch:**
> `{business}` — your site, your competitors, and an 8-second problem

*(Subject swaps to match `{top_finding}` — the point is it reads as specific to them, not as a mailshot. Alternatives: "had a look at {business}'s setup" / "{suburb} {trade} — quick thing I found")*

**Body:**

> Hi `{owner}`,
>
> I run Webnua — we build lead systems for `{trade}` businesses. Not pitching you blind: I actually went through `{business}`'s setup this week. Pulled the numbers, timed the site, checked your Google profile.
>
> Found three things costing you jobs. One's bigger than the other two.
>
> Rather than write you an essay, I put it on one page — what I found, and what we did about it. It's specific to you, takes about 90 seconds to read:
>
> **→ `{proof_page_url}`**
>
> No form, no signup. Just have a look.
>
> Craig
> Webnua

**Why it works:** "I actually went through your setup" + three specific things = not a mailshot. The link is the whole offer. The email stays under 90 words so the click is the only possible action.

---

### TOUCH 2 — Day 1 — SMS — Did it land?

Tradies live on SMS. This is often your highest-response touch. One line. Casual. Assumes they're busy.

> Hi `{owner}` — Craig from Webnua. Sent you a page yesterday breaking down `{business}`'s online setup + what we'd do about it. Worth a 90-second look when you're off the tools: `{proof_page_url}`

**Why it works:** SMS gets read. It's short enough to read at a red light. It re-delivers the link in the channel they actually check.

---

### TOUCH 3 — Day 3 — Email — Surface one element from the page

Don't say "did you see my email." Pull ONE thing out of the Proof Page and put it in the email body — give them a reason to go back. Rotate which element based on whether they opened (see trigger logic below). Default: the leak visual.

**Subject:**
> the bit most `{trade}` businesses miss

**Body:**

> `{owner}` — one thing from that page I'd flag if you only read one section.
>
> Every step between someone searching for a `{trade}` and you getting the booking is a place the job can leak out. Slow site, quiet Google profile, no after-hours capture — each one loses you a few. Most `{trade}` businesses are leaking at four or five steps at once and never see it, because a lost lead leaves no trace.
>
> The page shows your version of that — where `{business}` is leaking right now, and what it looks like sealed:
>
> **→ `{proof_page_url}`**
>
> The part people don't expect: we've already built the fix. It's not a proposal. It's a system that's running.
>
> Craig

**Why it works:** It teaches something true and useful *in the email* — so even a non-clicker gets value and stays warm. Then points back to the page for the personalised version.

---

### TOUCH 4 — Day 5 — PHONE — The warm call (ENGAGEMENT-TRIGGERED ONLY)

**This touch only fires if the prospect has shown an engagement signal:**
- Opened the Proof Page email AND/OR
- Visited the Proof Page (scroll tracking) AND/OR
- Clicked the call link but didn't complete a booking

**If no engagement signal by Day 5: skip this touch entirely.** The prospect continues to touch 5. Do not cold-dial non-engagers — it doesn't convert and it doesn't scale.

**The call is short and has one job: book the 15 minutes.** Not to sell on the call — to convert page-interest into a slot.

**Opener:**
> "Hi `{owner}`, it's Craig from Webnua — I sent over that breakdown of `{business}`'s online setup, the one-page thing. Caught you at an okay time for 30 seconds?"

**If yes — the pivot:**
> "I'll be quick. You had a look at the page — so you've seen we don't just point out what's wrong, we've actually built the system. The one number that's not on the page is the cost-per-lead, because honestly it reads as a lie in writing. The 15-minute call is where I screen-share the live dashboard and you see it for real. That's it — no pitch deck. Want me to grab you a slot this week?"

**If "send me something / not now":**
> "No worries — it's all on that page already, `{proof_page_url}`. The only thing the call adds is the live numbers. I'll text you a couple of times that'd work and you pick one. Sound fair?"
> *(Then immediately send a slot-options SMS — turn the soft no into a scheduled touch.)*

**If voicemail:**
> "Hi `{owner}`, Craig from Webnua — chasing the one-page breakdown I sent on `{business}`. No drama, I'll follow up by text. Page is `{proof_page_url}` if you want it in the meantime."
> *(Then send touch 5 SMS early, same day.)*

**Why it works:** The hardest part of cold outreach is getting a real human moment. By Day 5 with an engagement signal, this isn't cold — they've seen the page. The call just converts interest into a slot. Most competitors never pick up the phone; a tradie does, because their whole business runs on it.

---

### TOUCH 5 — Day 8 — SMS — The guarantee, in one line

Risk reversal is the single most persuasive lever for an audience that's been burned by agencies. One line.

> `{owner}` — the offer in one sentence: we build + run your whole lead system, €300/mo, and if it's not generating leads at the proven cost-per-lead in 90 days you get every cent back. Full breakdown's still here: `{proof_page_url}` — worth the 90 seconds.

**Why it works:** "Been burned before" is the #1 objection for this audience. The 90-day money-back guarantee answers it before they raise it. SMS delivers it where they'll actually see it.

---

### TOUCH 6 — Day 12 — Email — The "closing your file" pattern interrupt

Different energy. Most cadences just keep nudging. This one signals you're about to *stop* — which often gets the reply the nudges didn't.

**Subject:**
> closing `{business}`'s file — last check

**Body:**

> `{owner}` — I've reached out a few times about the lead system we built for `{business}`. No reply, which is a totally fair answer — you're busy and I'm a stranger in your inbox.
>
> I'm going to close your file so I'm not cluttering it up. Before I do, two honest possibilities:
>
> 1. It's just not relevant right now — in which case, no hard feelings, and I'll leave you be.
> 2. The page genuinely never got a proper look — in which case it'd be a shame, because it's specific to `{business}` and there's a real system sitting behind it.
>
> If it's #2, here it is one more time: **`{proof_page_url}`**
>
> If I don't hear back, I'll assume #1 and that's the last you'll hear from me.
>
> Craig

**Why it works:** Loss aversion + a clean out. "I'm about to stop" outperforms "please respond." It also genuinely respects their time, which is on-brand for the whole Webnua honest-operator positioning.

---

### TOUCH 7 — Day 16 — SMS — Genuinely final

Keep the promise from touch 6. One line. Then exit.

> `{owner}` — last one from me, as promised. If `{trade}` lead-gen ever moves up your list, the breakdown for `{business}` stays live here: `{proof_page_url}`. All the best with it — Craig.

**Why it works:** Keeping the "last one" promise is itself a credibility signal. A small number of people reply *to this exact message* because it's the first one with no ask in it. Then the prospect is retired.

## Engagement-trigger logic

The cadence has two paths running through it. The platform routes each prospect automatically based on signals.

### Signals the platform watches
- **Email open** (touch 1 or 3)
- **Proof Page visit** — the link was clicked and the page loaded
- **Proof Page scroll depth** — did they reach Act 2 / the proof section / the offer
- **Call-link click without booking** — strongest signal short of booking
- **Reply** on any channel — immediately exits the cadence into a human conversation

### How signals change the cadence

| Signal | What changes |
|---|---|
| **No engagement by Day 5** | Touch 4 (phone) is **skipped**. Prospect continues on email/SMS only. |
| **Any engagement by Day 5** | Touch 4 (phone) **fires**. This is the warm-call path. |
| **Proof Page visited, deep scroll, no booking** | Touch 4 phone is **prioritised** — call this prospect first in the day's call list. Touch 3 email swaps to the proof-section angle instead of the leak angle. |
| **Call-link clicked, no booking** | Skip ahead — call within 24 hours, don't wait for Day 5. This person is at the goal line. |
| **Any reply** | **Cadence stops.** Prospect moves to a live human conversation. The cadence's job is done the moment a real conversation starts. |
| **Booking made** | Cadence stops. Prospect is now a scheduled call. |

### The daily call list
Each morning the platform surfaces the engagement-triggered call list — everyone who hit a signal and is due touch 4 (or jumped the queue). This is the **only** list Craig cold-calls from. It's warm, it's short, and it's the highest-converting 30 minutes of the day.

## Who runs what

The cadence is **Craig + the semi-automated outreach platform**. The split matters — it's what makes the volume survivable.

### The platform does (no human time):
- Sends every templated email and SMS on schedule
- Tracks opens, visits, scroll depth, clicks
- Routes prospects down the engagement-triggered vs standard path
- Builds the daily warm-call list
- Stops the cadence on any reply or booking
- Logs everything back to the prospect record

### Craig does (the only human time):
1. **The Proof Page review** — per prospect, before they enter the cadence. The audit engine drafts it; Craig tightens it. This is the real throughput bottleneck (see below).
2. **The warm calls** — touch 4, from the daily triggered list only. ~15–30 mins/day at starting volume.
3. **The live conversations** — anyone who replies. This is the actual sales work and it's the good kind of work — they're already warm.

Everything else is the platform. Craig never manually sends a templated touch.

## The real bottleneck — and the honest volume plan

The cadence converts at a fixed rate. The thing that actually limits booked calls is **how many quality Proof Pages get built per day**, because every prospect needs one before they enter.

### The throughput maths

If a Proof Page takes Craig **10 minutes** of review (audit engine drafts it, Craig tightens hook + findings, confirms details):

| Proof Pages/day | Craig's review time | Prospects into cadence/day | Booked calls/day @ 2% |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 | ~5 hours | 30 | ~0.6 |
| 50 | ~8.5 hours | 50 | ~1 |
| 100 | ~17 hours | 100 | ~2 |
| 200 | ~33 hours | 200 | ~4 |

**Read that table honestly.** One person reviewing every Proof Page tops out at roughly **50/day before it eats the whole working day** — which is ~1 booked call/day. That's the real starting ceiling, and it's a fine place to start. But it means **"scale to 3–5/day" is not a volume-of-sending problem — it's a Proof-Page-throughput problem.** The sending is free. The review is not.

### The scaling ladder — which dial you actually turn

**Stage 1 — 1 call/day. Just Craig.**
- ~50 Proof Pages/day, ~8 hrs of Craig's day on review + calls + conversations.
- List source: the Places API prospect list you already have.
- This works now, with the tools you already have. No new hires, no new spend.

**Stage 2 — 2 calls/day. First constraint hits.**
- Needs ~100 Proof Pages/day = ~17 hrs of review. **One person can't.** This is the first real fork:
  - **Option A — a VA / appointment setter** does the first-pass Proof Page review against a checklist; Craig only spot-checks and approves. Cuts Craig's per-page time from 10 min to ~2 min. Now 100/day is ~3.5 hrs.
  - **Option B — tighten the audit engine** so its draft needs less human editing. Every minute shaved off review time is leverage that compounds at every volume level. This is the highest-ROI engineering work you can do.
- Realistically you do both.

**Stage 3 — 3–5 calls/day. It's a small operation.**
- Needs 150–250 Proof Pages/day. This is 1–2 people on Proof Page review + Craig on calls and closing, or a materially better audit engine, or both.
- The cadence is *still unchanged*. You are still only turning the volume dial. But the volume dial now has a person attached to it.

### The uncomfortable truth, stated plainly
3–5 booked calls a day is achievable, but not by one person alone — not because the cadence can't take the volume (it can, the sending is automated and free) but because **every prospect needs a quality Proof Page and that's human-reviewed work.** The path to 3–5/day runs through exactly two levers:
1. **Make the audit engine's draft good enough to need less review** (engineering — compounding leverage).
2. **Put a second person on Proof Page review** (hiring — linear leverage).

Anyone who tells you a cadence alone gets you there is selling you something. The cadence is necessary and it's built right. It is not sufficient. The volume engine underneath it is the other half, and at 1–2 calls/day you can run that volume engine yourself — which is exactly why 1–2/day is the right place to start.

## List sourcing — feeding the top

The cadence is only as good as what enters it. At each stage you need a reliable inflow of prospects who fit the ICP: owner-operated `{trade}` businesses, ~$200k–$3M revenue, English-speaking, already has *some* online presence (so there's something to audit).

**Stage 1 (50/day):** the Places API prospect list you already built covers this for months. Pull by trade + suburb, run audits, build pages.

**Stage 2 (100/day):** the Places API list still covers it, but you'll want to start widening — more suburbs, more trades, adjacent home-services categories. Begin tracking list burn rate so you don't run dry.

**Stage 3 (150–250/day):** now list sourcing is its own workstream — multiple Places API pulls, possibly a data provider, possibly the cold-outreach platform's own enrichment. This is a "later problem" but flag it now: at Stage 3 volume you'll burn through a metro area's worth of one trade in weeks.

**One rule at every stage:** never enter a prospect without a real, completed audit. A Proof Page with thin or generic findings is worse than no outreach — it burns the prospect and the channel. Quality of entry beats quantity of entry every time.

## Measuring it — what to watch

Don't measure this on booked calls alone for the first month. Watch the funnel, because the funnel tells you *which dial to turn*.

| Metric | What it tells you | Healthy-ish starting range |
|---|---|---|
| **Proof Pages built/day** | Your actual throughput ceiling | 30–50 solo |
| **Email open rate** (touch 1) | Is the subject line / sender working | 40%+ |
| **Proof Page visit rate** | Is the email body earning the click | 15–25% of sends |
| **Deep-scroll rate** | Is the page itself holding them | 40%+ of visitors |
| **Engagement-trigger rate** | How many qualify for the warm call | 20–30% of prospects |
| **Warm-call → booking rate** | Is the call converting interest to slots | 25–40% of calls |
| **Overall: prospect → booked call** | The number that sizes the throttle | aim 2%, 3%+ is great |
| **Reply rate (any channel)** | Cadence health overall | 5–10% |

**The diagnostic logic:**
- Low **open rate** → fix the subject line / sender reputation. Cheap fix.
- Good opens, low **visit rate** → the email body isn't earning the click. Fix touch 1 copy.
- Good visits, low **deep-scroll** → the Proof Page itself is losing them. Back to the page.
- Good scroll, low **booking** → the warm call or the offer needs work.
- Everything healthy, not enough calls → it's purely a volume problem. Turn the throttle.

That last line is the goal state: when every conversion rate is healthy and the only lever left is volume, the engine is built. Then scaling is just arithmetic.

## The one-page summary

1. **The cadence is fixed. Volume is the dial.** 7 touches, 16 days, email + SMS + triggered phone. Build once, scale by pouring more in the top.
2. **The Proof Page leads.** Touch 1 delivers the link. No warm-up phase — the artefact is the opener.
3. **The phone call is earned, not scheduled.** It fires on an engagement signal only. A warm call converts; a cold dial doesn't scale.
4. **Any reply ends the cadence.** The moment a real conversation starts, the cadence's job is done.
5. **The real bottleneck is Proof Page throughput, not sending.** Sending is automated and free. Review is human and finite.
6. **1–2 calls/day is a one-person operation.** 3–5/day needs either a sharper audit engine (engineering leverage) or a second reviewer (hiring leverage) — or both. The cadence doesn't change either way.
7. **Never enter a prospect without a real audit.** Quality of entry beats quantity, every time.
